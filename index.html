2. Hajj Mindset & Goal Setting

Our respected pilgrims: You must perform the rituals of Hajj for the glorification, admiration, devotion, and submission to Allah (The Most Glorified, the Most High). So, you perform them in a state of calmness and tranquility and by following the example of Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him and his family). Furthermore, all that must occupy your thoughts are the remembrance of Allah, Takbeer, praising of Allah, thanking of Allah, and asking Him for forgiveness. Keep in mind that you are in a state of worship from the moment you make the intention of wearing your ihram to the moment you successfully complete the required rituals.

Remember dear chosen pilgrim, that Hajj is not a casual stroll where you may play and have fun with no restrain, as you may see some people during Hajj or Umrah. There is always company that may distract you from the remembrance of Allah and pull you towards the disobedience against Him. And you may see some people playing excessively, laughing, and mocking the manners and forbidden actions, as though Hajj was ordered by Allah for the sake of having fun and playing.

There are three types of Hajj as follows:

HAJJ AL-TAMATT'U
  • Tamattu – linguistically means enjoyment. In this context, it means entering the state of Ihraam and then to exit from it after the completion of Umrah. This means freeing yourself from the restrictions of ihraam for some time until the 8th of Dul-Hajjah and then to enter it again for the purpose of Hajj.
  • It means to perform Umrah during the months of Hajj (months of Hajj are Shawwaal, Dhu'l-Qi'dah and Dhu'lHijjah). The pilgrim enters the state of Ihraam for Umrah. Once Umrah is completed, the pilgrim then exits the state of ihram.
  • The pilgrim enters the state of ihram by saying "Labayka Umratan" "O Allah! | answer Your call to perform Umrah". Then the pilgrim proceeds to Makkah and performs Tawaf, Sa'aee, shaves or cuts their hair and by doing that the pilgrim will exit the state of ihram.

Chapter 02

  • Then on the 8th of Dul-Hajjah the pilgrim enters the state of Ihraam again and proclaims the Hajj talbiyah by saying "Labayka Hajjan" "O Allah! I answer Your call to perform Hajj".
  • The pilgrim must sacrifice an animal.

The Farewell Tawaf(Tawafu Al-Wadaa)

Now that you are going to leave the sacred sites after completing all the rituals mentioned above, the time has come for the concluding ritual. At this time, you must offer farewell to the sacred mosque and refresh your eyes with the sight of the Kabah before departing. This is done by performing Tawaf AlWada (Farewell Tawaf), which you should do immediately before leaving Makkah.

Farewell Tawaf

This is called the Farewell Tawaf because by this a pilgrim bids farewell to Ka'bah, the House of Allah. In this there is no jogging (ramal), and it is the last rite performed by a nonresident pilgrim in Makkah before leaving for his home. Imam Malik (may Allah have mercy on him) has reported in AlMuwatta that 'Umar (may Allah be pleased with him) said, "The last rite of Hajj is tawaf around the Ka'bah."
